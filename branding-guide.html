06 — Voice & Tone
✓ Write like this
"Your web identity. Transformed in a week."
Direct, confident, specific benefit upfront.
"We take on 4 projects per month. Yours could be next."
Creates scarcity without being pushy.
"No back-and-forth. No delays. Just a site that works."
Addresses pain points directly.
"€3,997. 7 days. Yours."
Punchy, minimal, declarative.
✕ Avoid this
"We are a leading digital transformation agency leveraging synergistic solutions..."
Jargon-heavy, vague, no real meaning.
"We'd love to help you on your journey to success!"
Overly enthusiastic, empty language.
"Our team of passionate creatives delivers world-class experiences..."
Clichéd agency speak.
"Please don't hesitate to reach out to us at your convenience."
Weak, passive, wastes the reader's time.
07 — Usage Rules
✓ Do
  • Always write the brand name as "stfd studio" — all lowercase
  • Use Cormorant Garamond exclusively for all headings and display text
  • Maintain generous white space — let content breathe
  • Use hairline borders (1px, #E5E5E0) as the primary separator
  • Keep copy short and declarative — one idea per sentence
  • Use the green dot (#22C55E) only for status/availability indicators
  • Maintain the 4px border radius across all UI elements
✕ Don't
  • Never write "STFD", "Stfd", or "Stfd Studio" — always lowercase
  • Never use rounded corners larger than 9999px (pill) or 4px (sharp)
  • Never use colors outside the defined palette for decorative elements
  • Never use font weights above 600 for body text
  • Never add drop shadows to UI components — use borders instead
  • Never place text on low-contrast backgrounds without sufficient opacity
  • Never use more than 2 font families on any single page
